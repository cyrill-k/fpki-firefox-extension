F-PKI

F-PKI (Flexible Public-Key-Infrastructure) brings trust relationships to the internet.

Do you trust your best friend more than a stranger?

Trust is relative to the individual. But on the internet we trust everyone (i.e. every CA) the same, no matter the context.

F-PKI changes that!

Trust Preferences

Configure your individual trust preferences!

You trust your national CAs more than others? Any other trust preference?

Then tell your browser! (Here)

CA Compromise:

Let security experts decide, which CAs are the most trustworthy:

Install preconfigured trust settings, so that less secure CAs cannot override certificates issued by more trusted CAs.

Company CA:

Only your internal CA should be trusted for internal company domains, right?

Also, what if your internal CA gets hacked? Do you trust your internal CA to certificate websites outside of your company network?

Critical Websites:

Protect your critical communications by pinning the correct CA for a certain domain, so that no illegitimate CA can issue a valid certificate for that domain.

E.g. example.com uses CA xy for its SSL-Certificate. Then you should only accept certificates from CA xy for example.com, right?
